Technology news india

Artificial intelligence and IT:

Accenture plans to train 700,000 employees in Agentic AI, while India's IT sector faces a hiring slowdown due to global uncertainties. offers TCS has partnered with C-DAC to develop India's sovereign cloud ecosystem. Tamil Nadu launched TN SPARK, an initiative to train government school students in AI, coding, and robotics. Festive season offers:

Several companies like Samsung, Imagine, and Flipkart are rolling out attractive discounts and bundled offers on products like the Samsung Galaxy A06 5G, iPhone 17 series, and Nothing Phone 3. Imagine's pre-orders for the iPhone 17 series include assured benefits worth ₹17,000 and chances to win prizes up to ₹11 lakh, including a Maldives trip.


Startup funding

Sustainability homeware brand Ecosoul raised $5.2 million in debt funding, while fintech startup Pelocal secured $5 million in Series A funding to scale their payment orchestration platform on WhatsApp. Nothing, the smartphone maker, raised $200 million in Series C funding, achieving a valuation of $1.3 billion, with plans for AI-native devices.


New product launches:

Samsung launched the Galaxy S25 FE with special introductory offers including storage upgrades and cashback. Realme P3 Lite 5G was introduced, and Samsung's Galaxy Tab S10 Lite India prices were revealed. Apple Watch Series 11 and Ultra 3 will feature a new hypertension detection feature after FDA approval, though India availability is unconfirmed.
