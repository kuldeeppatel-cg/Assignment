Mutual Funds in India

The first mutual fund in India was introduced in 1963 with the launch of Unit Trust of India (UTI). The industry has evolved significantly, offering various schemes including equity, debt, and hybrid funds.

Mutual Fund Statistics

  • As of December 2023, the total AUM (Assets Under Management) stood at ₹50.78 trillion (~US$600 billion).
  • 73% of mutual fund units were redeemed within 2 years in FY 2022–23.
  • Mutual funds attracted 6% of household savings in FY2023, while 95% went to bank deposits and other traditional instruments.

Category-Wise AUM

  • Equity Funds: ₹20.33 lakh crore (Nov 2023)
  • Hybrid Funds: ₹6.90 lakh crore (Jan 2024)
  • Debt Funds: ₹11.97 trillion (Mar 2020)

Controversies

Several mutual funds in India have faced issues due to credit risk, mismanagement, or market volatility. High-profile cases include the Franklin Templeton fiasco in 2020 and problems with DHFL and UTI in earlier years.
